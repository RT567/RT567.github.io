Leadership Skills

I consider myself about 80% up the leadership scale (my own theory). Given the average person sits at 50% on this scale, it means that in a group of equally experienced peers, if I have the highest "leadership score", I'm likely to assume a leadership role. However, in a group of 8, someone else might score higher, in which case I'm more than happy to let them lead.

In my opinion, leadership should be viewed as a burden of service more than a position of elevated status. When I am in leadership positions, I feel responsible for the performance of the group as a whole and aim to allow every group member to contribute to their best ability. This means creating an environment where people do not feel left out or left behind and feel as though their opinions are truly heard.

Due to this understanding that I have of leadership, it also means I am an excellent team member and contributor even when not in a leadership position. I understand the difficulties and responsibilities and aim to provide succinct and meaningful information to my leader without overwhelming them with the details.


Teamwork Skills

I thrive in team settings. The opportunity to bounce ideas and energy off others is not only essential for producing remarkable results but also makes the journey enjoyable. "If you want to go fast, go alone; if you want to go far, go together."

I am a fair team member, understanding and never falling into any of the classic spiteful traps that can arise from communication breakdowns. I believe the first step to being a fair team member is being fair with oneself. I understand that some people are stronger in areas other than communication and teamwork (often highly technical individuals), and I aim to make their teamwork experience as easy and enjoyable as possible.

By no means am I a smooth-talking CEO character, but I believe that I possess a valuable combination of technical and interpersonal skills that make me a valuable and enjoyable team member to work with.
